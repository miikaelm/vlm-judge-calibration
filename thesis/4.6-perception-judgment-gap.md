# 4.6 The Perception-to-Judgment Gap

The two-experiment design allows a direct comparison between Experiment 1 (perceptual sensitivity: can the model see the error?) and Experiment 2 (edit judgment: does the model penalise the error?). For each matched stimulus, the score gap is defined as:

$$\text{Gap}(d, m) = \overline{\text{NM}_{E2}}_{d,m} - \overline{\text{sim}_{E1}}_{d,m}$$

where $\text{NM}_{E2}$ is the Experiment 2 `narrow_mean` (mean of `instruction_following`, `text_accuracy`, `visual_consistency`, `layout_preservation`) and $\text{sim}_{E1}$ is the Experiment 1 similarity score. `narrow_mean` is used as the primary gap metric because it is cross-model comparable.

A negative gap indicates that Experiment 2 `narrow_mean` is lower than Experiment 1 similarity score on the same stimuli, the model penalises the edit more harshly under the judgment task than its perceptual score would predict. A positive gap indicates the opposite. The cross-experiment Spearman correlation between similarity (Exp1) and `overall_quality` (Exp2) on matched stimuli quantifies global coherence between the two assessment modes. This correlation uses raw `overall_quality` deliberately: it is a within-model rank analysis, not a cross-model level comparison, so the scale offset does not affect interpretation.

### The NM gap story differs substantially from the OQ gap story

The most important observation from Table 4.13 is that the `narrow_mean` gap and the OQ gap tell different stories for most dimensions. The structural reason is the same one that makes `narrow_mean` more cross-model comparable: `text_accuracy` and `layout_preservation` stay near ceiling even when OQ collapses, which pushes NM gaps toward zero or positive values relative to OQ gaps. This means the NM gap measures whether `instruction_following` and `visual_consistency`, the discriminatory narrow dims, fall below or above Exp1 similarity, without the floor-collapsing effect of OQ's holistic judgment.

### Gemini: OQ amplification does not carry over to narrow_mean

Gemini's OQ score gaps are the most negative in the table, Gaussian noise (ŌłÆ1.96), blur (ŌłÆ1.05), font_style (ŌłÆ1.24), font_family (ŌłÆ0.88), and its per-dimension correlations are consistently significant. However, in `narrow_mean`, Gemini's gaps are positive for almost every dimension: blur +0.34, gaussian_noise ŌłÆ0.24, font_family +0.24, font_style ŌłÆ0.07, font_weight_heavy +0.26, font_weight_light +0.14, letter_spacing +0.49, opacity +0.07, position_offset +0.02, rotation +0.29, scale_error +0.47. Only gaussian_noise (ŌłÆ0.24), jpeg_compression (ŌłÆ0.23), and font_style (ŌłÆ0.07) have negative NM gaps.

This means Gemini's `narrow_mean` is at or above Exp1 similarity for most dimensions, the instruction-reasoning step does not penalise more harshly than Exp1 would predict, when measured through the four narrow dimensions. The severe OQ penalties Gemini applies (blur OQ 1.66, noise OQ 1.57) are a holistic-judgment effect: Gemini's "usability" assessment collapses for heavily degraded images, but `text_accuracy` and `layout_preservation` remain high, pulling NM up. The Žü(simŌåöOQ) correlations remain the best available per-dimension coherence measure, since they use OQ's fuller dynamic range.

### Qwen: reasoning-without-perception visible in narrow_mean

Qwen's NM gaps show the reasoning-without-perception pattern more clearly than any other model. For dimensions where Qwen is blind in Experiment 1, giving similarity scores near 5.0, it assigns NM scores of approximately 3.94ŌĆō4.24 in Experiment 2, producing negative NM gaps:

- **font_weight_light**: NM gap ŌłÆ1.06, Žü = n/s. Exp1 sim = 5.0 (zero variance, completely blind). Exp2 NM = 3.94.
- **font_weight_heavy**: NM gap ŌłÆ0.82, Žü = n/s. Exp1 sim Ōēł 5.0. Exp2 NM = 4.18.
- **font_style**: NM gap ŌłÆ0.78, Žü = n/s. Exp1 sim Ōēł 5.0. Exp2 NM = 4.22.
- **font_family**: NM gap ŌłÆ0.77, Žü = n/s. Exp1 sim Ōēł 4.94. Exp2 NM = 4.17.
- **position_offset**: NM gap ŌłÆ0.89, Žü = n/s. Exp1 sim Ōēł 4.98. Exp2 NM = 4.09.

The per-dimension Spearman correlations are non-significant for all of these (Žü = n/s), confirming that the NM variation is not grounded in perceptual signal. Qwen's Experiment 2 scores for these dimensions are responding to cues in the edit instruction even though the model demonstrably cannot see the relevant error in Experiment 1.

In contrast, for image-level degradations (blur, gaussian_noise), Qwen shows positive NM gaps (+0.12 and +0.22), with significant negative magnitude correlations. Here, Qwen's narrow_mean does track the degradation magnitude, and its Exp2 aggregate score is somewhat above Exp1 similarity, consistent with Qwen applying the edit instruction reasoning to confirm that blur and noise are visible failures.

### GPT-4o: JPEG compression inflation preserved in narrow_mean

GPT-4o's positive JPEG compression gap survives the transition to `narrow_mean`: NM gap = +0.64, OQ gap = +0.54. Both metrics give NM/OQ above Exp1 similarity (4.95 NM, 4.85 OQ vs Exp1 sim 4.31). This confirms that the suppression of GPT-4o's compression response is distributed across the narrow rubric dimensions, not concentrated only in the holistic OQ. When GPT-4o evaluates JPEG-compressed edit results, it rates both the narrow dimensions and the overall quality above what its perceptual comparison would predict.

### `color_offset` gap: positive for all models in narrow_mean

All three models show positive NM gaps for colour offset (Qwen +0.15, Gemini +0.30, GPT-4o +0.11), meaning Experiment 2 `narrow_mean` exceeds Experiment 1 similarity for colour-change stimuli. For Qwen this is a ceiling artefact (NM = 5.00, both experiments near ceiling). For Gemini, the positive NM gap (+0.30 vs OQ gap +0.05) reflects that instruction-level reasoning does not help detect colour errors, the NM stays high even though Gemini does show significant magnitude sensitivity for colour offset in NM (Žü = ŌłÆ0.563***). For GPT-4o, the NM gap is small (+0.11) and colour offset NM shows significant magnitude sensitivity (Žü = ŌłÆ0.445**).

## 4.6.3 Transition from Unreliable to Reliable Judgment: Magnitude Dependence

For dimensions where Experiment 2 OQ tracks magnitude (significant Spearman Žü, Table 4.13), the transition from unreliable to reliable judgment in Experiment 2 occurs at tier levels that are generally consistent with or later than the Experiment 1 thresholds established in Section 4.4. For example:

- Gemini's blur OQ mean: small 2.69 ŌåÆ medium 1.29 ŌåÆ large 1.07. The transition is abrupt between small and medium. Experiment 1 threshold was small tier (radius Ōēł1.3), and Experiment 2 OQ reaches near-floor also at small/medium.
- Qwen's Gaussian noise OQ: small 3.89 ŌåÆ medium 2.67 ŌåÆ large 1.94. Transition is gradual and reaches low-but-not-floor values at large.

For dimensions where Experiment 2 OQ does not track magnitude (non-significant Žü, Table 4.13), there is no transition, the model assigns a roughly constant quality score regardless of how severe the degradation is. These flat profiles confirm that the judgment task does not help models detect errors they miss in the perceptual task.

## 4.6.4 Summary: Gap Analysis Implications for RQ1b

The score gap analysis answers RQ1b at the experiment-level: the instruction-reasoning step in Experiment 2 does not systematically improve the reliability of detection compared to Experiment 1. Using `narrow_mean` as the primary metric:

- For **Gemini**, NM gaps are near-zero or positive on most dimensions. The instruction-reasoning step does not amplify penalties beyond what Exp1 similarity would predict in aggregate ŌĆö the harsh OQ penalties Gemini applies are a holistic-judgment effect not shared by the narrow dims.
- For **Qwen**, NM gaps are negative on dimensions Qwen cannot perceive (font weight, font style, position), confirming reasoning-without-perception: penalisation not grounded in visual evidence.
- For **GPT-4o**, the positive JPEG compression NM gap (+0.64) is the clearest anomaly: instruction context actively suppresses GPT-4o's response to compression artefacts across all narrow dimensions.

The OQ gaps (within-model reference) tell a more dramatic story for Gemini (ŌłÆ1.05 to ŌłÆ1.96 on image degradations) because OQ's holistic scope captures image-quality failures that the narrow dimensions might miss. Both metrics are reported in Table 4.13 and each is useful in its respective context.

The central implication is that the benchmark-facing task (Experiment 2) provides no additional sensitivity over the perceptual task (Experiment 1) for the dimensions that matter most, spatial position, scale, colour, and font properties, and introduces its own artefacts. A model that cannot see a colour error in Experiment 1 will not catch it in Experiment 2 either, and may assign high quality scores to colour-error stimuli while appearing to "follow" the instruction.
