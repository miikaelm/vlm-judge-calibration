# 4.5 Supporting Analyses

This section reports two supporting analyses that complement the main Experiment 2 results in Section 4.3: rubric internal consistency (Cronbach's alpha and intercorrelation structure) and inter-model agreement on Experiment 2 classifications. Neither analysis answers the research questions directly; they characterise the measurement properties of the rubric and the degree to which model judgments converge.

## 4.5.1 Rubric Internal Consistency and Intercorrelations

**Table 4.10. Spearman intercorrelations among Experiment 2 score dimensions and Cronbach's alpha, per model.**

| Dimension pair          | Qwen2.5-VL ρ | Gemini 3.1 Pro ρ | GPT-4o ρ |
|-------------------------|--------------|------------------|----------|
| IF ↔ text_acc           | 0.26         | 0.23             | 0.08     |
| IF ↔ visual_cons        | 0.47         | 0.35             | 0.39     |
| IF ↔ layout_pres        | 0.11         | 0.06             | 0.27     |
| IF ↔ OQ                 | 0.38         | **0.66**         | **0.77** |
| text_acc ↔ visual_cons  | 0.38         | 0.23             | 0.08     |
| text_acc ↔ OQ           | 0.37         | 0.23             | 0.08     |
| visual_cons ↔ OQ        | **0.72**     | **0.79**         | **0.80** |
| layout_pres ↔ OQ        | 0.15         | 0.55             | 0.55     |
| **Cronbach's α**        | **0.762**    | **0.765**        | **0.782**|

*IF = instruction_following; OQ = overall_quality (raw, within-model).*

Cronbach's alpha is approximately 0.76–0.78 for all three models, reflecting moderate but not high internal consistency. The rubric dimensions are neither fully redundant nor fully independent. Two structural patterns emerge from the intercorrelation matrix.

First, `visual_consistency` is the strongest single predictor of `overall_quality` across all models (ρ = 0.72–0.80). This is the dimension most directly measuring whether non-target properties of the image changed, that is, whether the degradation affected properties it should not have. The high correlation indicates that `overall_quality` is largely driven by `visual_consistency`. Which means visual_consistency carries most of the variance in OQ and including OQ in a composite with the narrow dimensions would double-count it, hence its exclusion from narrow_mean.

Second, `text_accuracy` and `layout_preservation` are weakly correlated with `overall_quality` for most models. `layout_preservation` is almost always rated 5 (Section 4.3.2), so its correlation with `overall_quality` is attenuated. `text_accuracy` is at ceiling (Section 4.3.2), adding essentially no variance. For Gemini, `layout_preservation` correlates more strongly with OQ (ρ = 0.55) because Gemini uses the dimension more dynamically.

Third, `instruction_following` correlates with OQ most strongly for Gemini and GPT-4o (ρ = 0.66 and 0.77 respectively), but more weakly for Qwen (ρ = 0.38). This is consistent with the Instruction-Not-Followed control result (Section 4.2.1): for Gemini and GPT-4o, `instruction_following` meaningfully predicts the edit quality assessment; for Qwen, OQ is partly decoupled from instruction adherence.
